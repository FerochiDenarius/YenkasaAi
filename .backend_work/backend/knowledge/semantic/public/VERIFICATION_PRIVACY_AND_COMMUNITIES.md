# Verification, Privacy, and Community Model

## Dual verification philosophy

Yenkasa uses a dual verification approach:

- details verification for basic identity and trust
- app verification for activity-based progression and rank

This means verification is not only about identity. It is also about earned participation inside the ecosystem.

## Details verification

The legacy algorithm document describes details verification as including:

- email verification
- phone verification
- stronger trust signals
- unlocks for posting or broader participation

## App verification and rank progression

The older design describes a phased system where users progress by meeting activity targets within a fixed period.

Key activity signals include:

- account age
- comments made
- followers or following activity
- likes achieved on a post
- daily logins
- ads viewed

The broader philosophy is that rank should reflect active ecosystem participation, not passive account ownership.

## Important ranking principle

The pitch deck emphasizes that Yenkasa prioritizes users who engage with others, not just users who collect passive attention. That means:

- following and interacting matter
- participation quality matters
- creator growth is linked to ecosystem contribution

## Community model

Communities are part of the core social architecture.

Communities affect:

- what content a user sees
- what local or interest-based discussions matter
- where a post becomes visible
- how users stay connected to real people and relevant contexts

## Privacy and control model

Yenkasa’s privacy model goes beyond simple user blocking.

The older design material describes:

- full blocking that hides posts and disables interaction
- community-level privacy controls
- post visibility controls by community
- controls over who can send messages
- user-controlled content visibility

Community-level blocking and filtering are important because they make privacy part of the social architecture, not just a settings page.
